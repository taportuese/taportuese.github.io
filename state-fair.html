MINNESOTA STATE FAIR INTERNSHIP

I had the once-in-a-lifetime experience of working in the state fair’s in-house sign shop the summer leading up to the 2017 Great Minnesota Get Together, the largest 12-day fair in the country, the Minnesota State Fair. Our design team of four worked on projects from ideation to design to production to installation. From stitching the banners I designed to mastering the X-Acto knife, I spent the summer honing my craft skills alongside design skills. Three of my biggest projects are elaborated on below.

THE GREAT MINNESOTA KNIT-TOGETHER

2017 was the first year a giant yarnbomb installation took over the Grandstand ramp, making it the state’s largest yarnbomb piece. I was in charge of creatinv a logotype for the piece, named “The Great Minnesota Knit-Together.” Its brand was expanded into floor decals that marked photo “opporu-knit-ies” with yarn-based puns on them.

MSF BRANDED SIGNAGE

I designed and produced several lines of signage using the Minnesota State Fair brand guidelines, including gate banners for every entrance to the fairgrounds and hand stamp reminder signs for pedestrian exits.

EMPLOYEE TIME CLOCK

Various shelter boxes and repurposed ATMs around the fairgrounds house Kronos systems for the thousands of fair-time staff members to clock in. We wanted to make their clock-in process easy and pleasant, so I created an infographic on how to clock in and check hours as well as eye-catching branding. We engineered the vinyl flaps on the boxes to protect the system and make them easy to spot for employees.
